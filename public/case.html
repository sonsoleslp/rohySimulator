Case Description

The patient experienced shortness of breath and left chest pain while cleaning at home. After resting alone at home for 3 hours, the symptoms did not disappear. So he went to the emergency room. He has clear consciousness and is able to engage in simple conversations. He was diagnosed with type 2 diabetes 3 years ago. He underwent tonsillectomy surgery when he was 20 years old. His father died of acute myocardial infarction 10 years ago. He is allergic to penicillin. He has a smoking history of about 51 years (started at the age of 18). He occasionally drinks alcohol and has no long-term drinking history. Insomnia and increased appetite. He only has 6 hours of sleep per day and his weak up time is usually at 6 AM. He ate more than 5 meals every day. He has a diet high in calories, frequently consuming fast food and desserts. His wife recently passed away, and the cat he raised with her for 13 years also passed away.
